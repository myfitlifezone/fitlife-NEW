Push-Ups

Start in a plank position with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower your chest toward the ground by bending your elbows, keeping your body straight. Push back up to the starting position, fully extending your arms. Repeat for 10-20 reps.

Did you know? Push-ups are a fantastic upper body exercise that primarily targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps, while also engaging the core and lower body for stability.

Bodyweight Squats

Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and toes slightly turned out. Push your hips back and lower your body as if sitting into a chair, keeping your chest lifted and knees behind your toes. Push through your heels to return to standing. Repeat for 15-20 reps.

Did you know? Bodyweight squats are a compound movement that targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves. They also engage the core for balance and stability.

Glute Bridges

Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart. Press through your heels and lift your hips towards the ceiling, squeezing your glutes at the top. Lower your hips back to the floor. Repeat for 15-20 reps.

Did you know? Glute bridges target the glutes and hamstrings while also engaging the core. They‚Äôre excellent for improving hip mobility and building strength in the posterior chain.

Bulgarian Split Squats

Stand a few feet in front of a bench or elevated surface. Place one foot on the bench behind you. Lower your body into a lunge position by bending your front knee, keeping your back straight. Push through the heel of your front foot to return to standing. Repeat for 10-15 reps per leg.

Did you know? Bulgarian split squats are an advanced variation of lunges that isolate each leg, building strength and stability in the quads, hamstrings, and glutes.

Planks

Start in a forearm plank position with your elbows directly under your shoulders and your body in a straight line. Engage your core and hold the position for 30-60 seconds, keeping your body as still as possible. Repeat for 2-3 sets.

Did you know? Planks are an excellent exercise for building core strength, stability, and endurance. They also engage the shoulders, glutes, and legs, making them a full-body exercise.
