Celery Sticks with Hummus

A crunchy, low-calorie snack paired with protein-rich hummus for a healthy and satisfying treat.

Did you know? Celery is super low in calories and high in water content, making it a great crunchy snack. Hummus, made from chickpeas, provides healthy fats and protein.

How to Prepare

  • 4-5 celery sticks
  • 2 tbsp hummus
  1. Slice the celery into sticks.
  2. Dip into hummus and enjoy!

Cucumber & Tomato Salad

A refreshing and hydrating salad rich in antioxidants and perfect for a quick snack or side dish.

Did you know? Cucumbers are hydrating and low in calories, while tomatoes are rich in antioxidants like lycopene, which promote heart health.

How to Prepare

  • 1 cucumber (sliced)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes (halved)
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • Pinch of salt and pepper
  1. Toss cucumber and tomatoes together in a bowl.
  2. Drizzle olive oil and season with salt and pepper.

Air-Popped Popcorn

A high-fiber, whole-grain snack that's super low in calories and perfect for weight loss.

Did you know? Air-popped popcorn is a whole grain, high in fiber, and very low in calories, making it a perfect snack for weight loss.

How to Prepare

  • 1/4 cup popcorn kernels
  • Pinch of salt (optional)
  1. Use an air-popper to pop the kernels or microwave them in a paper bag.
  2. Sprinkle with salt if desired.

Carrot & Cucumber Sticks with Greek Yogurt Dip

A hydrating snack packed with vitamins and a protein boost from Greek yogurt.

Did you know? Carrots and cucumbers are both low-calorie, high-water vegetables that help with hydration and digestion. Greek yogurt provides a protein boost that helps keep you full.

How to Prepare

  • 1 carrot (peeled and sliced into sticks)
  • ¬Ω cucumber (sliced)
  • 2 tbsp Greek yogurt
  • Pinch of salt and pepper
  1. Mix Greek yogurt with salt and pepper to taste.
  2. Dip carrot and cucumber sticks into the yogurt dip.

Apple Slices with Almond Butter

A sweet and satisfying snack combining fiber-rich apples with the healthy fats and protein of almond butter.

Did you know? Apples are high in fiber and antioxidants, while almond butter adds healthy fats and protein, helping you feel satisfied longer.

How to Prepare

  • 1 apple (sliced)
  • 1 tbsp almond butter
  1. Slice the apple and serve with a side of almond butter for dipping.
